<p><p>The Apple Pectin market is primarily categorized into two types: dry pectin and liquid pectin. Dry pectin is often favored for its longer shelf life and ease of storage, making it ideal for large-scale food production and home canning. Liquid pectin, on the other hand, is more convenient for smaller batches and is typically used in recipes requiring quick and easy thickening. Both forms serve essential roles in the food industry, especially in jams, jellies, and various fruit-based products.</p></p>
