<p><p>The esophageal metal stent market is categorized into three primary types: Nitinol, stainless steel, and titanium. Nitinol stents, made from a nickel-titanium alloy, offer flexibility and biocompatibility, making them suitable for complex anatomical areas. Stainless steel stents are known for their strength and lower cost but may be less flexible. Titanium stents combine lightweight properties with high resistance to corrosion, providing durability and compatibility with various medical applications. Each type addresses different patient needs and clinical scenarios.</p></p>
