<p><p>A float level switch is a device used to monitor and control the liquid levels in various applications such as tanks, reservoirs, and process vessels. It operates by using a buoyant float that rises or falls with the fluid level, triggering electrical contacts to signal the presence or absence of liquid at a specified level. <p><ul><li>Top-mounted Type</li><li>Side-Mounted Type</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The float level switch market comprises two primary types: top-mounted and side-mounted. Top-mounted switches are installed at the tank's top, allowing for easy access and maintenance while monitoring liquid levels from above. In contrast, side-mounted switches are fixed to the tank's side, ideal for narrow or tall tanks, providing accurate readings without taking up vertical space. Both types serve critical roles in applications like water treatment, oil and gas, and chemical storage, ensuring efficient and safe liquid management.</p></p>
