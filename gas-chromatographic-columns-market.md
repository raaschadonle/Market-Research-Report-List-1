<p><p>Gas chromatographic columns are essential components used in analytical chemistry for separating volatile compounds. They primarily fall into two categories: packed columns and capillary columns. Packed columns contain solid or liquid stationary phases within a tube and are typically used for high sample capacity and broad separation. In contrast, capillary columns have a narrow diameter and a thin stationary film, offering enhanced resolution and speed in separating complex mixtures. Both types cater to specific analytical needs in various industries.</p></p>
