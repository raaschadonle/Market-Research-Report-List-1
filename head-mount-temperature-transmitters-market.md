<p><p>Head-mount temperature transmitters are devices used for accurate temperature measurement in industrial applications. They generally use two main types of sensors: thermocouples, which measure temperature based on the voltage generated at the junction of two different metals, and Resistance Temperature Detectors (RTDs), which measure temperature by correlating resistance changes in a metal, typically platinum, with temperature variations. Both types offer distinct advantages in terms of response time, accuracy, and range, making them suitable for various monitoring and control processes.</p></p>
