<p><ul><li>External Risk Management</li><li>Internal Risk Management</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hospital Risk Management encompasses two primary market types: external and internal risk management. External risk management involves addressing potential threats from outside the organization, such as regulatory changes, natural disasters, and supplier risks, aiming to protect patient safety and financial stability. In contrast, internal risk management focuses on mitigating risks that arise within the hospital itself, including staff errors, equipment failures, and operational inefficiencies. Both strategies are essential for ensuring overall safety, compliance, and quality of care in healthcare institutions.</p></p>
