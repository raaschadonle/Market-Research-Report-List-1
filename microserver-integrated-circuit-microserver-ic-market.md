<p><p>Microserver Integrated Circuits (Microserver ICs) are specialized processors designed for low-power, high-density server applications. The market is primarily divided into two types: Intel-based and ARM-based microservers. Intel-based microservers leverage x86 architecture, offering robust performance and compatibility with existing software, making them popular in traditional data centers. In contrast, ARM-based microservers utilize RISC architecture, focusing on energy efficiency and scalability, catering to growing cloud and edge computing needs, while often targeting workloads that require high parallel processing capabilities.</p></p>
