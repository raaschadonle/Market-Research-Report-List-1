<p><p>The optical preclinical imaging devices market encompasses various techniques, primarily fluorescence and bioluminescence imaging. Fluorescence imaging utilizes fluorescent dyes or proteins to visualize specific tissues or cells in real-time, allowing researchers to track biological processes with high spatial resolution. In contrast, bioluminescence imaging relies on the emission of light from biochemical reactions, typically involving luciferase enzymes, to monitor cellular activity and disease progression. Both techniques are crucial for advancing drug discovery, disease research, and personalized medicine in preclinical studies.</p></p>
