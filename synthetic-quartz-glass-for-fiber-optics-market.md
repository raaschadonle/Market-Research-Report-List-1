<p><ul><li>CVD Process</li><li>Plasma Deposition</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Synthetic quartz glass for fiber optics is produced primarily through two methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Plasma Deposition. The CVD process involves the chemical transformation of gaseous precursors into solid silica, enabling the formation of high-purity glass with excellent optical properties. Meanwhile, Plasma Deposition utilizes plasma to deposit a thin layer of silica, offering enhanced uniformity and control over glass characteristics. Both methods are essential for producing high-performance optical fibers that are critical in telecommunications and data transmission applications.</p></p>
