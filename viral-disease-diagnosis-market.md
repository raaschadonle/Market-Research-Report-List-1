<p><p>The viral disease diagnosis market comprises three main types: serodiagnostic tests, specimen examination, and viral isolation. Serodiagnostic tests detect specific antibodies or antigens in the blood, offering insights into a patient's immune response to viral infections. Specimen examination involves analyzing clinical samples, such as blood or swabs, for the presence of viral pathogens. Viral isolation entails culturing viruses from patient specimens, enabling definitive identification and characterization. Together, these methods enhance the accuracy and effectiveness of viral disease diagnosis.</p></p>
