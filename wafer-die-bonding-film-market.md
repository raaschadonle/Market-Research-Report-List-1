<p><p>The wafer die bonding film market is categorized into two main types: non-conductive and conductive. Non-conductive die bonding films are primarily used to insulate and protect sensitive semiconductor components, offering excellent thermal stability and low moisture absorption. In contrast, conductive die bonding films facilitate electrical connections between die and substrates, enhancing performance in high-speed applications. Both types are essential for ensuring reliable bonding in semiconductor packaging, catering to different applications depending on the required electrical properties and thermal performance.</p></p>
